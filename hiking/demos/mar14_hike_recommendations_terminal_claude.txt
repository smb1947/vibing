  1/1. Mount Townsend Trail

  (Only viable option this week — see note below)

  Location: Olympic National Forest, Quilcene area (eastern Olympic Peninsula)
  Distance: 8.6 mi | Elevation Gain: ~2,900 ft | Difficulty: Hard
  Estimated Duration: ~6–6.5 hrs (AllTrails) | Rating: 4.8★ (2,743 reviews)

  Access

  - Drive from Greenlake: ~2.5 hrs via ferry (Edmonds–Kingston → US-101 → Quilcene → FR 27/2909)
  - Road: Mostly paved with gravel sections — sedan-friendly in clear conditions; AWD recommended for mid-March upper road (~3,300 ft). If snow blocks FR
   2909, park at lower trailhead (~2,400 ft) and add ~2 mi round trip.
  - Chains: Not confirmed required, but prudent to carry. Road is passable without chains in recent snow per historical reports; verify with Quilcene
  Ranger Station: (360) 765-2200
  - Road confidence: Medium — no USFS closure notice found for FR 27/2909 (note: FR 29 IS closed, but that's a different road)

  Logistics

  - Pass Required: Northwest Forest Pass ($5/day or America the Beautiful)
  - Group Limit: 12 (Buckhorn Wilderness)
  - Wilderness Permit: Self-issue at trailhead (free, day hike only)

  Conditions for 2026-03-14

  - Hazards: None confirmed. NWAC forecast for Olympics zone unavailable this far out — check https://nwac.us/avalanche-forecast/ March 12–13.
  Considerable avalanche risk expected on summit slopes above ~5,000 ft after this week's 14–20" snowfall. Carry avalanche awareness; route-finding above
   5,000 ft will require judgment.
  - Weather: High ~40°F at Quilcene (~28–32°F at summit) | Precip: 60% | Wind: 15–25 mph valley, summit gusts higher | Post-frontal clearing — storm
  moves through March 8–13, March 14 is post-storm
  - Trail: Conditions unverified (no WTA report in 14 days — check https://www.wta.org/go-hiking/hikes/mount-townsend directly). Snow likely from ~3,500
  ft upward; if using upper trailhead (~3,300 ft), expect snow at or near the car. Microspikes required; snowshoes strongly recommended for upper section
   and summit ridge.
  - Snow Level: Storm: ~1,500 ft dropping; by March 14 post-frontal: forecast to rise to ~3,000–4,000 ft (unconfirmed — marginal for upper trailhead at
  3,300 ft). ⚠ Marginal snow risk at upper trailhead. If precip or snow level forecast worsens by March 12, reassess.

  Highlights

  360° panoramic views of the Olympics, Hood Canal, and Cascades. Open ridge traverse with dramatic scenery. Dense old-growth forest on the lower portion
   — beautiful in any weather. Iconic eastern Olympics summit.

  Why This Trail on This Day

  The "March Madness" multi-foot Cascade snow event (March 8–13) eliminates every Cascade trail in your filter set this weekend — through road closures,
  weather extremes, or avalanche hazard. Mount Townsend on the Olympic Peninsula is the one trail that escapes the worst of the storm thanks to the
  eastern Olympics rain shadow, with 60% precip vs 100% everywhere in the Cascades. It adds a new region (Olympics, not visited in 2026), a summit
  character different from your recent ridge-viewpoint and lake hikes, and at 2,900 ft of gain it's a solid progression step. The post-storm window on
  Saturday gives the best chance of a clearing sky and possible summit views.
