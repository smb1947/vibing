**HeyLily Launches AI Assistant to Intercept and Screen Deepfake Scam Calls**
*AI-powered “Lily” assistant screens unknown calls for fraud and voice clones before the phone ever rings*

**SEATTLE – \[March 11, 2026\]** – Today, HeyLily launched an AI-powered call-screening assistant that shields families from the surge in AI-powered fraud attacks targeting older Americans. HeyLily provides peace of mind by automatically screening unknown calls for both scam tactics and AI voice clones in real time and only connecting safe callers to the user.

Older Americans are facing a newer kind of phone scam: a caller could sound exactly like someone trusted. Call blocking and spam filters help reduce obvious junk, but they cannot stop live impersonation from new or spoofed numbers. Americans still received an average of **2.56 billion scam robocalls per month** from January to September 2025, and **only 44%** of phone companies had fully installed mandated robocall-fighting controls as of late September 2025\. In 2024, consumers reported **more than $12.5 billion** in fraud losses, including **$2.95 billion** from imposter scams. AI voice cloning raises the stakes because it is easy to weaponize. One security lab reported creating a convincing voice clone from **just three seconds of audio**. In July 2025, a Florida mother reported losing **$15,000** after a caller used an AI-cloned version of her daughter’s voice to demand bail money. People cannot reliably spot high-quality deepfakes. Researchers found that only **24.5%** of “well done” deepfake videos were recognized as fake, even when viewers knew to look for fakes. Today’s protections may catch known scam numbers or suspicious requests, but they do not verify whether the **voice itself** is real, leaving families exposed when the scam sounds like someone they trust. 

HeyLily addresses these unmet needs by automatically intercepting calls from unknown numbers to screen for both scam tactics and AI voice clones before the user ever answers. When an unknown call arrives, the phone remains silent as Lily, the AI assistant, answers and evaluates two signals in real time: (1) language patterns that indicate fraud tactics such as urgency or secrecy, and (2) audio signals that suggest the voice may be synthetic or cloned. If high risk is detected, HeyLily automatically hangs up on the caller, protecting the user from ever being exposed to the threat. For legitimate calls, the phone rings with a distinctive “Safe Call” ringtone and provides a brief audio whisper summarizing the caller’s identity and intent before the user speaks. For added security, every call is logged with a transcript and risk analysis that can be shared with anyone.

“Scam calls are no longer just about unknown numbers—they are about believable impersonation,” said Anson Choi, Head of Design at HeyLily. “Families need protection that stops the threat before the phone even rings. HeyLily intercepts these high-pressure moments automatically, so by the time you answer, you already have the clarity and control to handle the call safely.”

HeyLily is designed to work in the exact moments scams succeed. Customers download the app and enable Lily to intercept unknown callers by default. When a call comes in, Lily answers instantly, asking the caller to state their name and purpose. Behind the scenes, the system calculates the likelihood of fraud and AI cloning. If a risk is identified, Lily terminates the call and notifies the user later. If the caller is legitimate, the user is alerted with a green, high-contrast screen and a brief audio summary in their earpiece: “This is a human caller. They say they are calling from the Pharmacy.” This eliminates the dread of scam calls and allows the user to start the conversation with confidence. HeyLily also provides a full log of every interaction, including transcripts and tactic analysis, which can optionally be shared with trusted contacts.

“Before HeyLily, the hardest part was how real the voice sounded because panic takes over fast,” said Mei Xiao, an owner of a small sushi franchise. “Now the phone doesn’t even ring if it’s a scammer, and when it does, I get a clear whisper telling me exactly who is calling before I even say hello.”

“We’ve invested in call blocking for years, but the most damaging scams don’t look like spam. They sound like trust,” said Evan Lam, a student at UW. “HeyLily adds the missing layer by screening out voice cloning and only letting through the calls that are actually safe.”

Giving them an AI assistant to handle the screening makes a world of difference,” said Jeisson Estrada, Business Analyst at Amazon. “HeyLily handles the pressure by flagging those scam tactics automatically and only connecting the calls that matter.”
